About Capybara

The capybara is a semiaquatic mammal and the world's largest rodent.

Binomial name

Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris

Distribution

South America

Diet

Grass, leaves, fruit, seeds, bark, etc.

Length

110-130 cm

Weight

40-50 kg

Body

Covered with stiff brown hair, no tail and short limbs.

Capybara is very large and fat like a pig, but it is actually a member of the mouse family. Rats are classified as rodents, and among them, they are the largest rats that currently exist on earth.

They live in grasslands and marshes along river banks from Panama in South America to northeastern Argentina.

They spend the day in the shade of bushes or swimming in water to avoid the heat. Unlike other rodents, capybaras do not build burrows. He sleeps comfortably in a naturally formed depression in the ground with grass and leaves. Then, in the evening when the heat subsides, I eat again and sleep slowly at night.

In contrast to wild capybaras, zoo capybaras do not have enemies, so their lives are centered around feeding time. Many zoos feed the capybaras in the morning right after the park opens, so if you go to see the capybara right after the park opens, you will already be nervous.

Capybaras bask in the sun until evening after eating. And just before the park closes, we will start activities again if we are hungry. So when you usually go to the zoo to see capybaras, you get the impression that they are motionless and lazy to see them boating or napping.

Therefore, ponds in capybara farms are always a subtle color. However, there seems to be an exception at the zoo to the basics written here. On cold days, they secretly decide to use the edge of the breeding hut as a toilet, and they mistake a puddle for a swamp after it rains.

It may be less relevant in a zoo breeding state where there are no enemies.

Capybaras live in groups of 10 to 20 animals on average. A strong male at the center forms a family with several females and their offspring.

During the dry season, many groups live in lakes and marshes where there is still water, so the group may temporarily exceed 100 animals, but when the rainy season comes again, large groups disperse return to their original small group life.
